Hardware meets intelligence

I'm Krushna Balasaheb Dahatonde — a final-year Electronics & Telecommunication Engineering student with a deep interest in embedded systems, VLSI, and wireless communication. I love the challenge of turning abstract circuit theory and signal math into systems that actually work in the real world.

My project work spans IoT health monitors with cloud dashboards, FPGA-based digital filters for real-time audio, and a CNN-based deep learning model for 5G NR channel estimation. Each project has pushed me to go deeper — from optimizing Verilog MAC units to simulating 50,000+ fading channel samples in MATLAB.

During my internship at EVER Electronics, Pune, I contributed to ARM Cortex-M firmware and ran hardware-in-loop tests — my first real taste of production-grade embedded development. I want to keep building at that intersection of firmware, hardware, and algorithms.
